The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a numerical scale used to communicate the quality of the air to the public. It provides an easy-to-understand indicator of how polluted the air currently is or how polluted it is forecasted to become. The AQI ranges from 0 to 500, with higher values indicating higher levels of air pollution and greater health risks.

Causes Of Air Pollution

Industrial Emissions

Factories and power plants release large amounts of pollutants, including sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter.

Vehicle Emissions

Cars, trucks, and buses emit carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, and hydrocarbons, contributing significantly to urban air pollution.

Burning Fossil Fuels

The combustion of coal, oil, and natural gas for energy and transportation releases numerous pollutants into the air.

Agricultural Activities

Pesticides, fertilizers, and methane emissions from livestock contribute to air pollution.

Natural Sources

Wildfires, volcanic eruptions, and dust storms also release pollutants into the atmosphere.

Effects Of Air Pollution

Respiratory Disorders

Causes conditions like asthma, bronchitis, and other chronic respiratory diseases.

Cardiovascular Problems

Increases the risk of heart disease and stroke.

Lung Cancer

Long-term exposure to polluted air can lead to lung cancer.

Environmental Damage

Leads to acid rain, which can harm ecosystems and structures.

Climate Change

Contributes to global warming by increasing the concentration of greenhouse gases.

Prevention Methods

Reduce Vehicle Use: Promote public transportation, cycling, and walking to reduce emissions from private vehicles.

Use Cleaner Energy Sources: Shift from fossil fuels to renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydro power.

Industrial Regulations: Implement stricter emission standards and use cleaner production technologies in industries.

Enhance Green Spaces: Plant trees and maintain green spaces to absorb pollutants and improve air quality.

Raise Awareness: Educate the public about the sources and effects of air pollution and ways to reduce their personal carbon footprint.
